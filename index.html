Jenny Blyth Fine Art is based at Carey Blyth Gallery

8 Woodstock Road Oxford OX2 6HT

Gallery Hours : Wed-Sat from 12-5 pm

Facebook & Instagram @careyblythgallery

Located in the heart of Oxford, Carey Blyth Gallery shows contemporary art for sale by a stable of local, national and international artists in solo and mixed exhibitions.

'I find that I am chasing beauty and trust that you will find something beautiful in the gallery to savour for years to come.  I have a profound love of the natural world and much of the art that I exhibit is grounded in the tradition of landscape and still life whether abstracted or figurative.'   Jenny Blyth, curator & gallerist.

July 2023 : Evocations : Summer Exhibition of paintings by Chloe Fremantle, and more..

Beautiful summer paintings that reference colourfield, surrealism and abstraction.  Chloe Fremantle is a member of the Royal Watercolour Society and has been painting and exhibiting internationally for forty years with work in international Collections. It is the artists of the 1930s and 40s who have been the most influential for Chloe Fremantle - Colour Field, Surrealist and Abstract artists such as Patrick Heron, Eileen Agar, Helen Frankenthaler, as well as Henri Matisse, Braque and Picasso, clearly her muses. Chloe has lived in New York, London and Italy, and now splits her studio time between London and the Alpes Maritime on the Cote d'Azur.  Her paintings capture the light and joy of the Mediterranean imparting a sense of calm that she achieves despite the complexity of her compositions . The exhibition presents a selection of paintings from the 2022 London exhibition Light Seeking Light curated by Jenny Blyth at the Royal Watercolour Society, with paintings by Netta Carey, Anne Lynch, and ceramics by Alan Berman.   

BACKGROUND : 

Jenny Blyth has twenty five years experience in contemporary art as curator and gallerist. She was Curator at Saatchi Gallery 1990-2002 and ran a floating gallery for ten years thereafter, originating projects and mounting exhibitions largely in London, Manchester and Oxford. Jenny Blyth Fine Art (JBFA) curated 40 exhibitions at Art Jericho in Oxford between 2010 and 2017, and opened Carey Blyth Gallery in September 2019. 

JBFA Curatorial Projects outside of CBG Gallery include Light Seeking Light : Paintings by Chloe Fremantle & Peter Blegvad at the Royal Water Colour Society Galleries, at Whitcomb Street WC2 (October/November 2022) Seaside Citadels : Paintings by Day Bowman at Bermondsey Project Space, London SE1 (June 2021); Contemplation : Snow Into Water  (September/October 2020) and Touching the Earth (May 2019) at North Wall Arts Centre, Oxford; and an exhibition of photography by Jenny Blyth Reflections in the Landscape at Wolfson College, Oxford (February 2019).
